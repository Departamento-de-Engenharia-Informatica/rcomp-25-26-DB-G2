# General technical decisions

#### Main Decisions: ####

* **Backbone Cables:** All backbone cables use optic fiber monomode 10 due to its immunity to dispersion, resulting in higher data rates and extended cable lengths.

* **Ethernet Network Cables:** For all other Ethernet network cables, Category 7 (CAT7) will be employed. CAT7 surpasses CAT6 in overall performance, offering higher data rates—up to 10 Gbps compared to CAT6's limit of 1 Gbps. Additionally, CAT7 cables feature superior shielding, enhancing reliability by reducing susceptibility to signal loss or disruption caused by electromagnetic interference (EMI). In the context of a large infrastructure like the one discussed later, where data transmission occurs over extended distances in a potentially noisy environment, CAT7 emerges as the optimal choice.

* **Cable Standards:** All cables will adhere to the 568A standard, as it is the most prevalent standard in the market and boasts high compatibility with other standards.

* **Patch Panels:** Each building will be equipped with patch panels utilizing ISO 8877 female connectors, serving structured cabling purposes.
